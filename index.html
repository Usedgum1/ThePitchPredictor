How The Projection Works

Projected Ks are a matchup-aware estimate, not a blind guess.

We study how a pitcher has performed in similar spots before, then layer in the context around tonight's game: the expected lineup, how often those hitters swing and miss, the umpire behind the plate, the weather, the park, and the pitcher's recent form and workload. It’s a lot, and it matters.

We don't stop there. We also run multiple statistical models so one outlier or strange outing doesn’t distort the projection. That helps us balance different signals, account for variation, and turn a messy pile of baseball context into one clear projected Ks number. The goal is to be informed.
